We encompasses all the individuals who are engaged in physical or mental work, including employees, workers, and laborers. What manpower does can vary significantly depending on the context and the specific roles and responsibilities of individuals within the workforce.

Construction Worker

Involved in building structures such as houses, roads, bridges, and commercial buildings.

Carpenter

Works with wood to construct, install, or repair structures and fixtures. They may build furniture, framework, or frameworks for buildings.

Electrician

Installs, maintains, and repairs electrical systems in homes, businesses, and industrial settings. They work with wiring, circuits, and electrical equipment.

Plumber

Installs and repairs plumbing systems, including pipes, fixtures, and appliances. They work on water supply, drainage, and sewage systems.

Weldder

Uses equipment to join metal parts together. They work in industries such as construction, manufacturing, and automotive repair.

Mason

Works with materials like brick, stone, and concrete to build structures, including walls, chimneys, and sidewalks.

Roof Technician

Installs and repairs roofs on residential and commercial buildings. They work with various roofing materials like shingles, tiles, and metal.

Landscaper or Groundskeeper

Maintains outdoor spaces by mowing lawns, trimming trees and shrubs, and planting flowers.

Janitor or Custodian

Responsible for cleaning and maintaining the cleanliness of buildings, including offices, schools, and hospitals.

Warehouse Worker

Handles the storage and distribution of goods. They may be involved in tasks like packing, loading/unloading, and operating forklifts.

Delivery Driver

Transports goods and products to customers or between locations. They may work for courier services, delivery companies, or retail stores.

Farm Laborer

Performs various tasks on farms, such as planting crops, harvesting, and caring for animals.

Factory Worker

Operates machinery and assembly lines to produce goods. They may be involved in manufacturing processes across various industries.

Auto Mechanic

Repairs and maintains automobiles, diagnosing and fixing issues with engines, brakes, and other vehicle components.

Garbage Collector

Picks up and disposes of waste and recyclables from residential and commercial areas.
